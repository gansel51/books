Fiction Favorites

Fantasy

  • The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson

    An epic beginning to the Stormlight Archive series with immersive worldbuilding and complex characters who face moral dilemmas in a war-torn world.

  • The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss

    A beautifully written tale of Kvothe, a gifted young man who becomes the most notorious wizard his world has ever seen.

  • Jonathan Strange & Mr. Norrell by Susanna Clarke

    A historical fantasy set in 19th-century England with a masterful blend of magic, history, and Regency-era politics.

Science Fiction

  • The Three Body Problem by Cixin Liu

    A mind-bending exploration of first contact with an alien civilization, interweaving physics, philosophy, and Chinese history.

  • Project Hail Mary by Andy Weir

    An astronaut wakes up alone on a spacecraft with no memory, tasked with saving humanity from extinction.

  • Hyperion by Dan Simmons

    A Canterbury Tales-style narrative following seven pilgrims on a journey to the mysterious Time Tombs and the enigmatic Shrike.

Historical Fiction

  • The Orphanmaster's Son by Adam Johnson

    A Pulitzer Prize-winning novel set in North Korea that explores themes of propaganda, identity, and survival under totalitarianism.

  • Wolf Hall by Hilary Mantel

    A richly detailed reimagining of Thomas Cromwell's rise to power in the court of Henry VIII.

  • The Thousand Autumns of Jacob de Zoet by David Mitchell

    A Dutch clerk's experiences in 18th-century Japan, combining historical accuracy with elements of the supernatural.

Non-Fiction Selections

Science & Philosophy

  • Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind by Yuval Noah Harari

    A thought-provoking examination of the history of our species from the Stone Age to the present.

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman

    Nobel Prize winner Kahneman's exploration of the two systems that drive how we think and make decisions.

Memoirs & Biographies

  • Educated by Tara Westover

    A remarkable memoir about growing up in a survivalist family and the transformative power of education.

  • The Glass Castle by Jeannette Walls

    A stunning memoir of resilience and redemption, and a revelatory look into a family both deeply dysfunctional and uniquely vibrant.

Spring Reading

Fresh beginnings and renewal:

Summer Reading

Perfect for vacation adventures:

  • Where the Crawdads Sing by Delia Owens

Autumn Reading

As the shadows lengthen:

Reading Challenge Suggestions

Looking to expand your literary horizons? Try these reading challenges:

Around the World Challenge

Read books from authors representing different countries:

  • Japan: "Convenience Store Woman" by Sayaka Murata
  • Nigeria: "Americanah" by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie
  • Colombia: "One Hundred Years of Solitude" by Gabriel García Márquez

Literary Time Machine

Read one significant work from each century:

  • 18th Century: "Robinson Crusoe" by Daniel Defoe
  • 19th Century: "Jane Eyre" by Charlotte Brontë
  • 20th Century: "To the Lighthouse" by Virginia Woolf
  • 21st Century: "Cloud Atlas" by David Mitchell
