Fiction Favorites

Fantasy

  • The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson

    An epic beginning to the Stormlight Archive series with immersive worldbuilding and complex characters who face moral dilemmas in a war-torn world.

  • The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss

    A beautifully written tale of Kvothe, a gifted young man who becomes the most notorious wizard his world has ever seen.

  • Fledgeling by Octavia Butler

    A unique take on the vampire genre, exploring themes of identity, race, and community through the story of a young amnesiac vampire discovering her extraordinary heritage.

Science Fiction

  • The Three Body Problem by Cixin Liu

    A mind-bending exploration of first contact with an alien civilization, interweaving physics, philosophy, and Chinese history.

  • Project Hail Mary by Andy Weir

    An astronaut wakes up alone on a spacecraft with no memory, tasked with saving humanity from extinction.

  • Red Rising by Pierce Brown

    In this dystopian science fiction series set on Mars, Darrow finds out his caste has been betrayed and that the lives of generations of his family have been wasted.

Historical Fiction

  • The Orphanmaster's Son by Adam Johnson

    A Pulitzer Prize-winning novel set in North Korea that explores themes of propaganda, identity, and survival under totalitarianism.

  • The Things They Carried by Tim O'Brien

    A powerful collection of linked short stories about a platoon of American soldiers in the Vietnam War, blending fact and fiction to explore the burdens of war.

  • A Gentleman In Moscow by Amor Towles

    A charming novel about a Russian count sentenced to house arrest in a grand hotel, exploring themes of resilience and the passage of time.

Non-Fiction Selections

Histories

  • The Wager by David Grann

    A gripping account of a shipwreck and mutiny in the 18th century, exploring themes of survival and human nature.

  • Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman

    Nobel Prize winner Kahneman's exploration of the two systems that drive how we think and make decisions.

Memoirs & Biographies

  • Educated by Tara Westover

    A remarkable memoir about growing up in a survivalist family and the transformative power of education.

  • The Last Lecture by Randy Pausch

    A poignant and inspiring lecture by a terminally ill professor, sharing life lessons and the importance of pursuing dreams.

True Crime

  • In Cold Blood by Truman Capote

    A pioneering work of true crime that explores the brutal 1959 murders of a Kansas family and the subsequent investigation, blending journalism and literary storytelling.

  • The Innocent Man by John Grisham

    A gripping account of a wrongful conviction in Oklahoma, revealing the flaws in the justice system.

Reading Challenge Suggestions

Looking to expand your literary horizons? Try these reading challenges:

Around the World Challenge

Read books from authors representing different countries:

  • Spain: "The Shadow of the Wind" by Carlos Ruiz Zafón
  • Germany: "The Book Thief" by Markus Zusak
  • Japan: "Never Let Me Go" by Kazuo Ishiguro
  • Algeria: "The Plague" by Albert Camus
  • Russia: "The Master and Margarita" by Mikhail Bulgakov (Russia)
  • Brazil: "The Alchemist" by Paulo Coelho
  • China: "The Joy Luck Club" by Amy Tan

Literary Time Machine

Read one significant work from each century:

  • 18th Century: "Robinson Crusoe" by Daniel Defoe
  • 19th Century: "Moby Dick" by Herman Melville
  • 20th Century: "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald
  • 21st Century: "The Overstory" by Richard Powers
